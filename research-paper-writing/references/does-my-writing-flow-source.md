# Writing Flow Note for HPC Papers

One of the most frequent questions writers ask themselves or others is "does my writing flow?" For HPC papers, that usually means "can a reviewer follow the workload, bottleneck, system design, and evidence without getting lost?" If you find yourself concerned about the flow of your writing, use these strategies.

1. Pretend you are the reader of your paper.

   - Often when we write, our work makes sense to us because we created it. However, HPC papers often mix workload definitions, platform assumptions, and performance claims, and that can make the argument hard to follow for outsiders.
   - By looking at your paper from the perspective of a reviewer, you are checking whether each paragraph has one message and whether the logic is explicit.
   - Questions to ask:
     1. Would a reviewer understand the workload and platform without hidden context?
     2. How do the body paragraphs connect back to the bottleneck and the contribution?
     3. Would someone not on the project understand why the system is faster or more scalable?
2. Reverse outlining

   - Reverse outlining breaks an already written section into thesis, topic sentences, and supporting evidence.
   - Here is how to reverse outline:
     1. Write down the main claim of the section.
     2. Write down each topic sentence of each paragraph.
     3. Also write down the main evidence or explanation used within your paragraphs.
     4. Check every topic sentence and make sure it is clearly related to the section claim.
     5. Check all evidence and make sure it supports the paragraph topic.
     6. If a topic sentence does not clearly relate to the claim, revise it or remove it.
   - If it is easy to write a reverse outline, then it is a good sign that your section is well organized.
3. Add headings and sections to your paper.

   - If you are not ready to make a complete outline, add headings and subsections to expose structure.
   - By splitting a paper into sections and sub-sections with headings, the paper is telling the reader what each part is about.
   - For HPC papers, this is especially useful for showing system model, method, experiments, and scalability analysis.
4. Use transitions or "flow" keywords.

   - Using appropriate transition words improves the flow of your paper. These words act as guide posts, indicating the relationship between thoughts, sentences, or paragraphs.
   - Useful transitions for HPC writing include cause/effect, contrast, and consequence words such as `therefore`, `however`, `as a result`, `in contrast`, and `thus`.
